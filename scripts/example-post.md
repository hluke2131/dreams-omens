Flying dreams stay with you. Even years later, people can describe the exact feeling — the lift, the freedom, sometimes the wobble of almost falling. They are rarely random.

## Why flying dreams feel so significant

Most dream imagery is borrowed from waking life. Flying is not. It is one of the few experiences your dreaming mind constructs entirely on its own, and that originality is part of why it lands so hard.

## A few things flying dreams often mean

### 1. A sense of release from something

Often the flight begins right after a difficult chapter ends — leaving a job, finishing a project, walking out of a relationship that had grown too small. The lift in the dream mirrors the lift you are quietly experiencing in your life.

### 2. A bid for perspective

Flying dreams almost always change the angle of vision: you see your house, your town, the landscape from above. Your mind may be trying to step back from something that has felt overwhelming up close.

### 3. Confidence you have not fully claimed yet

In some traditions, flying represents the part of you that knows it can — even if your waking self has not yet acted on it. Worth asking what you would do if you trusted yourself the way the dream self does.
